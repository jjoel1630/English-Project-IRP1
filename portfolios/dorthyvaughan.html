Experience

1932-1943

Mathematics Teacher

Robert Russa Moton High School

As a teacher at Robert Russa Moton High School, I taught the young students the core concepts of mathematics. I helped develop curriculum and implement this curriculum to teach the students to the best of my abilities.

1943-1971

Mathematician and Programmer

Langley Research Center

As a mathematician and programmer, I helped run tests on aircraft, create complex and useful math formulas, and I contributed to the World War II effort.

1949-1961

Director of West Area Computing

Langley Research Center

As the first African American Female Director of West Area Computing, I helped supervise a group of engineers and mathematicians. I helped mentor my colleagues and taught them new programming languages as well as other concepts to help them grow. I also helped transition my team to work with the new machine computers.

1961-1971

Director of Electronic Computing

Langley Flight Mechanics Division

As a Director of Electronic Computing, I learned new programming languages such as FORTRAN, and helped teach these languages to my colleagues.
